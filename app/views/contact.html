Add a touch of the historically high yen, and by November, the company issued a warning to investors it would end this fiscal year with a $1.2 billion loss, prompting Moody’s to downgrade its credit rating. Today, that estimate nearly doubled. As Stringer told Bloomberg Businessweek: “You can’t keep saying ‘I had this and I had that.’ When the Thailand floods hit, I thought, well wait a minute… You don’t feel sorry for yourself, but you do occasionally say, if some of my competition had the same experience…”

It's a complex editing conundrum. It will be the main story tomorrow, so should have significant coverage in the paper, but whatever we report will be out of date by 10am. Then we need a package for the web as soon as possible after 10am regardless of which way the story goes. If Huhne is found guilty we'll want to cover the political ramifications, the possibility of a reshuffle as well as the very human dimension which centres on something essentially very sad: the breakdown of a marriage. And if it comes to writing about his political downfall - he will certainly resign if charged - we have to do it without prejudicing any criminal case. Our lawyers are going to be as busy as our reporters.
